About

Why I do what I do.

My path into data wasn't planned — and that's exactly what makes it valuable. I started in door-to-door sales, where I learned what it takes to generate leads, read people, and hit targets under pressure. But I'm going to keep it real — I hated how inefficient I was.

So I started building systems to fix it. What began as curiosity about SQL turned into designing full data warehouses from scratch — ingesting raw CRM and ERP data, modeling Star Schemas, and automating quality checks that replaced hours of manual Excel cleanup. Through this, I realized that there was a world of opportunity in data engineering.

Today I'm studying Information Systems at the University of Florida while building real data engineering projects — from cloud pipelines on Azure to enterprise warehouse architectures. My sales background and my love for business are my biggest edge. I don't just move data from point A to point B. I understand why it matters when it gets there.
